About

My name is Rob McGuire-Dale, and I recently graduated from Oregon State University with a B.S. in computer science. I am currently working as an Cyber Security Assessor (A.K.A. penetration tester, or white hat hacker) for Boeing Information Security.

In the past, I have worked as a software developer for The Open Source Lab, and a film editor/animation instructor at The NW Film Center of the Portland Art Museum.

I am interested in the more-social aspects of computer science, e.g., open source software and community development, the web as a computing platform, human-computer interaction and cyber security.

I prefer working with Linux, and favorite languages are Python and JavaScript. I am also familiar with many other languages, frameworks, tools, and technologies such as Django, jQuery, Git, AJAX, HTML, CSS, Drupal, Java, C/C++, PHP, and Subversion (SVN).

Summer of 2010, I gave a talk at Open Source Bridge about a project I was heavily involved with called 'TouchScreen', an open- source interactive kiosk framework.

For more details, please see my résumé. Please feel free to contact me if you have any questions or comments.
